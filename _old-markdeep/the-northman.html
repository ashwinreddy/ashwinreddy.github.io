**_The Northman_** We stood a while in a quiet circle outside the theater before I realized they weren't fans. To break the silence, I scattered a defense, then a friend replied: if _The Northman_ is a successor to _Hamlet_, as you say, it removes too much of interest to stand up on its own. True. And yet it moves. Two years later, I have my defense. The story of _Hamlet_ goes back to a legend recorded by twelfth century historian Saxo Grammaticus: when his uncle Feng murders his father Aurvandill for the crown and marries his mother Gerutha, prince Amleth feigns madness while he plots his revenge. It is this telling that Shakespeare used for his play and director Robert Eggers and cowriter Sjón return to for their film. The risk, evidently, is they may seem to revise the bard, but they are saved in using the same source toward a decidedly different end. I take _Hamlet_ to be chiefly about modern man's crisis of faith. Written around 1600, the play establishes Hamlet as a student at the University of Wittenberg, where Martin Luther set off the Reformation the previous century. The interim saw great religious and intellectual unrest as England and the Catholic Church cleaved apart. Indeed, the play was often put on for Oxbridge students, who had to contend with the results. No less so Hamlet. [Connective Tissue]. Hamlet commits to abandoning all past knowledge and turning to vengeance after he sees his father's ghost. > Yea, from the table of my memory
> I'll wipe away all trivial fond records,
> All saws of books, all forms, all pressures past
> That youth and observation copied there
> And thy commandment all alone shall live
> Within the book and volume of my brain
> Unmixed with baser matter. He's unwilling to abandon the book metaphor. Shortly after, he says the famous line, "There are more things in heaven and earth, Horatio, / Than are dreamt of in your philosophy." All of which suggests a kind of reasoning for one's self. Both these statements, though, undercut themselves. They are highly dramatic statements. To talk about commandments and continuing the metaphor of the book, sola scriptura. A more elegantly metaphysical sentence cannot be devised. Thrust from an old Danish legend into this new secular age, the prince no longer knows when he is. > The time is out of joint; O cursed spite
> That ever I was born to set it right! > And, in fact, Hamlet will be unable to hold to his promise. He's always questioning and thinking, thinking and questioning. The prince's attitude is skeptical and stoical. Satan, in this vein tutored by Hamlet, proclaims to have > A mind not to be chang'd by Place or Time.
> The mind is its own place, and in itself
> Can make a Heav'n of Hell, a Hell of Heav'n. > These are in part problems of culture. There must be a way to be detached from vicissitude without denying circumstance. Tightly bound to time and place, this is precisely what the _The Northman_ shows. Lead actor and producer Alexander Skarsgård, who pitched the film to Eggers, sought to bring the myths of his homeland to the screen. Eggers' earlier entries _The Witch_ and _The Lighthouse_ show an obsessive interest in both historical realism in the sets, dialogue, and costuming juxtaposed with elements of folklore and myth in plot and atmosphere. A clear example is in naming, which is out of place in _Hamlet_. Francisco and Bernardo have Spanish names, Laertes and Ophelia are Greek. _The Northman_, however, recalls the old names. The king is Aurvandil. The queen is Gudrún. The brother is Fjölnr. Ophelia becomes Olga. But also obviously in showing Viking relics and brutality, something film is just better equipped to show than a play. There is one regard in which Hamlet and Amleth are similar, and that is Hamlet post-act 5. Hamlet finds action but during an offstage sea change. Amleth takes fate seriously and his visions have as much reality as anything in the frame. So he just needs to plan and wait. Everything in its ordained time. The religion also includes magic. Amleth doesn't need to understand the details, he just needs to make it work. The film's biggest misstep is reintroducing the Freudian reading by having the mother seduce Amleth. It is altogether fitting that the prince himself should highlight this most important use of screen and stage. > Good my lord, will you see the players well bestowed? Do you hear, let them be well used, for they are the abstract and brief chronicles of the time. >
