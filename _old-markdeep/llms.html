**Enlarging Language Models** This is a potted history of large language models like GPT and Claude and more specifically, the Transformer architecture that powers them. (#) $n$-grams As I've mentioned [elsewhere](llms-babel.html), Claude Shannon was an early pioneer of language modeling. His approach, published in 1948, was to tabulate the frequency of letters or words given the preceding $n$ letters or words, respectively, and then sample from this table. (#) Recurrent Neural Networks A recurrent neural network is designed to accept a variable-length sequence $x$ as input. A hidden state $h_t$. $$ h_t = f(x_t, h_{t-1}) $$ The network learns to store any necessary and relevant information about the past in $h_t$. But RNNs suffer from the **vanishing gradient problem**. The gradient tends toward zero, so no update is made. (#) LSTM Long short-term memory. Somewhat complicated machinery that lets the network "forget" and "overwrite" cell data. (#) seq2seq (#) Transformer The Transformer architecture was introduced in a paper titled "Attention Is All You Need" [#Vaswani17]. That paper specifically tried to do translation from English to German and French. network parallelizes and is more efficient.
